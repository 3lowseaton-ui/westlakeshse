HSE Helpline

Monthly retainer — 12 month contract

£50/month

The HSE Helpline gives businesses straightforward, affordable access to professional health and safety advice without the commitment of a full management service. Ideal for small businesses that need reliable guidance on hand but do not require regular site visits or documentation support.

What's included

  • ✓Phone, WhatsApp and email access for day-to-day HSE queries including legal questions, incident guidance, contractor queries and general compliance questions
  • ✓Responses within 24 hours on weekdays and 48 hours at weekends and bank holidays. Clients will be notified in advance of any change during planned annual leave
  • ✓Access to a shared Google Drive folder of standard HSE templates including risk assessment templates, incident report forms and toolbox talk sheets

  • ✓Proactive contact at month 11 to discuss contract renewal and any changes to requirements

12 month minimum term. One month's written notice required to terminate after the initial term.

HSE Partner

Named Competent Person — 12 month contract

£250/month

The HSE Partner service provides a formally appointed named competent person as required under Regulation 7 of the Management of Health and Safety at Work Regulations 1999. Ideal for small to medium, low-to-medium risk businesses such as offices, retail, light trade and small manufacturing operations.

What's included

  • ✓Formal written appointment of West Lakes HSE as Named Competent Person, documented via a signed appointment letter retained on file by both parties
  • ✓Phone, WhatsApp and email access — responses within 24 hours on weekdays and 48 hours at weekends. Clients notified in advance of changes during annual leave
  • ✓Full review and update of the Health & Safety Policy annually, or complete rewrite at contract start if required
  • ✓Up to 5 risk assessments per year — written from scratch or existing assessments reviewed and updated
  • ✓One site audit per year with verbal debrief and written findings within 10 working days
  • ✓Incident investigation guidance and RIDDOR reporting support by phone and email
  • ✓Monthly WhatsApp check-in to maintain open communication and address emerging concerns
  • ✓Access to shared Google Drive folder of standard HSE template documents
  • ✓Proactive contact at month 11 to discuss renewal and any changes to requirements

Please note

  • • Site visits at weekends or by prior arrangement — minimum two weeks' notice required
  • • This is a supported competent person arrangement, not a full-time or emergency HSE management service
  • • In the event of a serious incident, immediate remote guidance will be provided and a site visit arranged at the earliest opportunity
  • • This service does not include emergency on-site attendance

12 month minimum term. One month's written notice required to terminate after the initial term.

HSE Review

One-off fixed price — no contract required

£300–£500
fixed price

A clear, honest picture of where your business stands against its legal health and safety obligations — with a prioritised action plan to move forward. Ideal for businesses that have never had an independent HSE review, those who have recently taken on staff, or any organisation wanting reassurance the basics are properly in place.

Small premises

Office, small retail, under 10 staff

£300–£350

Medium premises

Workshop, warehouse, 10–30 staff

£400–£500

What's included

  • ✓Pre-visit questionnaire completed by the client — allows a targeted audit and makes the best use of time on the day (approximately 15–20 minutes to complete)
  • ✓On-site audit on a mutually agreed weekend — half day for smaller premises, full day for larger sites
  • ✓Review of existing documentation including policies, risk assessments, training records and incident records
  • ✓Verbal debrief at the end of the visit covering key findings
  • ✓Formal RAG-rated written report within 10 working days — Red (immediate action), Amber (action within defined timescale), Green (satisfactory)
  • ✓One follow-up call within two weeks of the report to discuss findings and answer questions (approximately 30 minutes)

Following the Review, a tailored proposal for any follow-on support can be provided on request. There is no obligation to proceed.

HSE Accreditation

Project-based — no contract required

From £100
+ project work

Many businesses in construction, facilities management, utilities and related sectors are required to hold a recognised health and safety accreditation. West Lakes HSE supports businesses through the application process for the main SSIP-approved schemes including SafeContractor, CHAS, Constructionline, SMAS and Acclaim.

Pricing — agreed before any work begins

  • • Initial gap analysis: £100
  • • Project work to complete the application: £150–£500 depending on scheme and documentation required
  • • All project costs confirmed in writing before work begins — no hidden costs

What's included

  • ✓Initial questionnaire and gap analysis to establish what documentation is already in place and what is outstanding
  • ✓Review of existing documentation against the requirements of the chosen scheme
  • ✓Written summary of findings and fixed-price quote for remaining work — provided before any further commitment
  • ✓Development of any missing documentation — risk assessments, method statements, policies, environmental policies and training records as required
  • ✓Submission of the completed application to the accreditation body on behalf of the client
  • ✓Support with any queries or remedial actions raised by the accreditation body following submission

Documentation is prepared during evenings and weekends — turnaround typically 5–10 working days per document. The accreditation decision rests with the scheme provider. West Lakes HSE will support any remedial actions required following submission.

2. What This Policy Covers

This policy explains how West Lakes HSE collects, uses, stores and protects personal data. It applies to all clients, prospective clients, suppliers and website visitors.

This policy complies with the UK General Data Protection Regulation (UK GDPR) and the Data Protection Act 2018.

3. What Personal Data Is Collected

West Lakes HSE may collect and process the following personal data:

  • Name, job title and contact details (email, phone, postal address)
  • Business name and company information
  • Information provided during consultancy engagements, including workplace health, safety and environmental data
  • Financial and billing information necessary to fulfil a contract
  • Correspondence and communications (emails, letters, meeting notes)
  • Any other information voluntarily provided by the client or contact

4. How Personal Data Is Collected

Personal data is collected in the following ways:

  • Directly from the individual when making contact, requesting a quote, or engaging services
  • During the course of providing consultancy services
  • Through email, telephone or written correspondence
  • Via the West Lakes HSE website or online enquiry forms

5. Legal Basis for Processing

Personal data is only processed where there is a lawful basis to do so under UK GDPR Article 6:

  • Contract — processing is necessary to perform a contract or to take steps at the request of the individual before entering into one
  • Legal Obligation — processing is necessary to comply with applicable legal obligations (e.g. tax records, health and safety legislation)
  • Legitimate Interests — processing is necessary for the legitimate business interests of West Lakes HSE, where those interests are not overridden by the rights of the individual
  • Consent — where explicit consent has been obtained (e.g. for marketing communications)

6. How Personal Data Is Used

  • Delivering consultancy services
  • Responding to enquiries and managing client relationships
  • Preparing reports, risk assessments, policies and other deliverables
  • Invoicing and processing payments
  • Complying with legal and regulatory obligations
  • Sending relevant updates or information about services, where consent has been given

7. Who Personal Data Is Shared With

West Lakes HSE does not sell, rent or trade personal data. It may be shared with:

  • Trusted third-party service providers (e.g. accountancy, IT support), who are bound by confidentiality and data protection obligations
  • Regulatory bodies or authorities where required by law (e.g. HMRC, the Health and Safety Executive)
  • Any party to whom explicit permission has been given

8. How Long Data Is Kept

  • Client records and HSE documentation: 7 years from end of engagement (in line with HMRC requirements)
  • Enquiry and pre-contract correspondence: 12 months if no contract was entered into
  • Marketing consent records: until consent is withdrawn or a removal request is made

9. Individual Rights

Under UK GDPR, individuals have the following rights:

  • Right of Access — to request a copy of the personal data held
  • Right to Rectification — to request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data
  • Right to Erasure — to request deletion of personal data in certain circumstances
  • Right to Restrict Processing — to request that use of personal data be limited
  • Right to Data Portability — to receive personal data in a structured, machine-readable format
  • Right to Object — to object to processing based on legitimate interests or for direct marketing
  • Right to Withdraw Consent — where processing is based on consent, it may be withdrawn at any time

To exercise any of these rights, please contact West Lakes HSE using the details in Section 1. A response will be provided within one calendar month. There is no charge for making a request.

10. Data Security

West Lakes HSE takes the security of personal data seriously and has appropriate technical and organisational measures in place to protect it from unauthorised access, loss, misuse or disclosure.

In the unlikely event of a personal data breach, the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) will be notified within 72 hours and, where required, affected individuals will be informed directly.

11. International Data Transfers

West Lakes HSE does not routinely transfer personal data outside the UK. If this becomes necessary, appropriate safeguards will be put in place in accordance with UK GDPR requirements.

12. How to Complain

Any concerns about how personal data has been handled should be raised with West Lakes HSE in the first instance using the contact details in Section 1.

Individuals also have the right to lodge a complaint with the UK's supervisory authority:

  • Information Commissioner's Office (ICO)
  • Website: www.ico.org.uk
  • Helpline: 0303 123 1113
  • Address: Wycliffe House, Water Lane, Wilmslow, Cheshire, SK9 5AF

13. Changes to This Policy

This Privacy Policy may be updated from time to time. The current version will always be available on request and on this website. Any significant changes will be communicated to active clients directly.
